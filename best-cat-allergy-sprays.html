Why Specificity Matters

Scientific evidence shows that allergen mitigation is most effective when the treatment binds or neutralizes the specific allergen protein (Fel d 1) rather than just cleaning surfaces. Generic sprays may reduce surface allergens but do not stop airborne triggers and may contribute to side effects like respiratory irritation.

Choosing the Right Spray for Your Home

  • For targeted allergy relief: Choose sprays designed specifically for Fel d 1 (like Pacagen).
  • For routine cleaning: Regular surface cleaners can help, but should not substitute allergen neutralizers.
  • For sensitive households: Avoid harsh disinfectants that may irritate skin or lungs.

How to Use Cat Allergy Sprays Safely

  1. Spray in well-ventilated areas.
  2. Focus on high-contact zones (furniture, bedding, carpet).
  3. Avoid spraying directly on cats unless product is formulated for that use.
  4. Use in conjunction with regular cleaning and grooming.
