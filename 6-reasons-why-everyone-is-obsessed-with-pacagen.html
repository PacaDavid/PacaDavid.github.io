What makes cat allergies so hard to control?

Most people assume cat hair is the main culprit. In reality, the bigger issue is usually a protein allergen commonly spread through dander and saliva, then transferred onto fur, bedding, upholstery, and other high-contact surfaces around the home.

That means even when your home looks clean, active allergens can still be hanging around where your family relaxes the most. And because these particles are easily redistributed, many pet owners feel like they are always cleaning but never really getting ahead of the problem.
